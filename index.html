About Me

My name is Yash Patel, and I am currently pursuing a Bachelor of Science in Data Science at Northeastern University’s Khoury College of Computer Sciences, with minors in Economics and Mathematics. What excites me most about this field is that it sits at the intersection of logic, creativity, and real-world impact. I enjoy working on technical problems that require both analytical thinking and communication, whether that means designing a pipeline, training a model, or translating results into something that helps a team make better decisions. My coursework in algorithms, database design, large-scale information storage, probability, statistics, and advanced data science programming has given me a strong foundation, but I have learned just as much from applying those ideas outside the classroom.

Through internships and personal projects, I have grown especially interested in data engineering and machine learning. At the New York Mets, I worked on travel and parking analytics by building automated pipelines, validation workflows, and predictive models that supported the fan experience. Earlier roles at Rapid Finance and Batoi Systems helped me see how machine learning and automation can solve very different problems, from customer segmentation to job listing aggregation. Beyond academics and internships, I stay involved through the John Martinson Honors Program, DATA Club, and BAPS Campus Fellowship, where I serve as president. Those experiences have shaped how I lead: I value discipline, collaboration, and building things that are thoughtful as well as technically sound.
