How I build and what I care about

My path so far has combined technical depth, curiosity about people and systems, and a genuine interest in using data to improve everyday experiences.

Experience and Projects

One of the most meaningful professional experiences on my resume is my Data Engineer internship with the New York Mets. In that role, I worked on a problem that was both technically challenging and highly practical: understanding how fans move to and from the stadium, and how data can improve that experience. I designed and deployed a Dockerized pipeline using Python, Google Cloud Scheduler, Google Maps APIs, and BigQuery to gather travel information from more than 25,000 landmarks across 2,500 zip codes. I also built validation workflows to improve trust in production data and contributed machine learning models that predicted parking wait times. What I liked most about that work was the full pipeline perspective. It was not just about writing code or training a model in isolation. It was about building something reliable enough to support dashboards, decision-making, and even communication in the Mets’ fan emails.

My earlier internships taught me different sides of applied data work. At Rapid Finance, I used clustering methods such as k-means and DBSCAN to identify customer behavior patterns in financial transaction data, which improved segmentation quality. At Batoi Systems, I focused more on software engineering and automation by building AI-driven web scraping tools for a job platform. Outside internships, I enjoy projects that mix machine learning with subjects I personally care about. Ask Vachanamrut stands out because it combined retrieval-augmented generation, semantic search, and faith-centered content in a way that felt technically interesting and personally meaningful. Other projects, like forecasting arbitrage opportunities in foreign exchange markets or predicting MBTA Green Line travel reliability, reflect the same pattern in my work: I like taking large, noisy datasets and shaping them into systems that answer a real question. That approach also connects to my broader interests in AI, sports, geocaching, national parks, and even interpretive writing, all of which push me to look closely, ask better questions, and tell clearer stories.
